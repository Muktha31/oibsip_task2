Ratan Tata

1937-2024

"I don't believe in taking right decisions. I take decisions and make them right."

Biography

Ratan Tata, born on December 28, 1937, is a prominent Indian industrialist and philanthropist, best known for his leadership of the Tata Group, one of India's largest conglomerates. After graduating from Cornell University and completing an advanced management program at Harvard Business School, he became chairman of Tata Sons in 1991, driving the company's global expansion and overseeing notable acquisitions like Jaguar Land Rover and Corus Steel. Ratan Tata is also recognized for his commitment to social responsibility, directing significant profits towards healthcare, education, and rural development initiatives, making him a revered figure in both the business world and society at large.
